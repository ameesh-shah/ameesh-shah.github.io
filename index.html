About Me

Hi! I'm currently a First Year Ph.D student at UC Berkeley, where I'm advised by Sanjit Seshia.

My research interests primarily revolve around how we can make machine learning more trustworthy in real-world applications. I see trustworthiness as an effort in improving ML in a variety of ways, such as interpretability, verifiability, or robustness. In order to pursue these improvements, I aim to solve machine learning problems with solutions that incorporate concepts from formal methods. In doing so, I hope to marry the expressive power of state-of-the-art machine learning with the precise, deterministic properties of formal methods techniques. Please see my research for some examples of this intersectional work if you're interested.

I received my Bachelor's and Master's degrees from Rice University, where I worked primarily at the intersection of program synthesis and machine learning. I was advised by Swarat Chaudhuri.. I also worked on projects with Ankit Patel. and Richard Baraniuk. I was fortunate to be supported by the Rice CS Graduate Fellowship, and I enjoyed contributing to the "Understanding the World Through Code" NSF Expeditions Project.

I've also spent time at Microsoft Research AI, where I worked with the GRAIL group on interactive machine-learning based program synthesis.

In my free time, I enjoy doing lot of things, including but not limited to: running, playing tennis, buying sneakers, cooking, generally vibing, and watching my beloved Cleveland sports teams lose year after year.
